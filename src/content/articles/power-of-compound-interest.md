## What is Compound Interest? (Interest on Interest)

**Compound Interest** is the process where you earn interest not just on your original investment (principal), but also on the interest that has already accumulated.

From an audit perspective, simple interest is a **linear** progression, while compound interest is an **exponential** acceleration.

### Real-World Audit: $10,000 Investment at 8% for 30 Years

| Year | Simple Interest Growth | Compound Interest Growth | The "Compounding Bonus" |
|------|-----------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| 1 | $10,800 | $10,800 | $0 |
| 10 | $18,000 | $21,589 | +$3,589 |
| 20 | $26,000 | $46,610 | +$20,610 |
| **30** | **$34,000** | **$100,627** | **+$66,627** |

**The Verdict:** By choosing a compound growth vehicle, your money earns **2.8x more** compared to simple interest.

## Compound Interest Formula (The Math Explained)

To audit your own growth, you must understand the underlying mechanics.

### The Standard Formula:

$$A = P \left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}$$

**Where:**
* **A** = Final Amount (The target wealth)
* **P** = Principal (Your initial investment)
* **r** = Annual interest rate (Decimal form, e.g., 0.08 for 8%)
* **n** = Compounding frequency (Times per year)
* **t** = Time (Total years)

## The Rule of 72: The Auditor's Shortcut

During a quick financial review, I use the **Rule of 72** to estimate doubling time without a calculator.

**Formula:** $72 \div \text{Interest Rate} = \text{Years to Double}$



### Quick Reference Table:
| Interest Rate | Years to Double | $10k becomes $80k in... |

## The 3 Silent Killers Destroying Your Compound Growth

Even with a perfect calculator, your real-world returns are under constant attack. In my audit reports, I call these the **"Wealth Leaks."**

### 1. Inflation: The Invisible Erosion
If your money compounds at 8% but inflation is 3%, your **Real ROI** is only about 4.85%. Over 30 years, $1 million in 2026 will likely have the purchasing power of only $400,000 in today's money.

### 2. Management Fees: Death by a Thousand Cuts
A "small" 1.5% annual fee sounds harmless. However, over 30 years, that fee can consume **over 35% of your total gains**. 
* **Auditor's Advice:** Always check the **Expense Ratio** of your funds. Anything above 0.50% requires a strict justification.

### 3. Tax Drag: The Compounding Saboteur
If you pay taxes on your gains every year (in a taxable brokerage account), you lose the ability to compound that "tax money." 
* **The Fix:** Maximize tax-advantaged accounts like **401(k) or IRA** to let your gross returns compound fully.
